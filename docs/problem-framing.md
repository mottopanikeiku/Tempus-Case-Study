# Problem Framing

## Target User

Field sales representative preparing for an oncology account meeting with:

- a treating oncologist
- a pathology or lab leader
- a medical director or program lead

## Job To Be Done

Before a short account meeting, help the rep answer four questions quickly:

1. Who should I prioritize this week?
2. Why does this provider matter right now?
3. What concern is most likely to come up?
4. What is the clearest next thing to say or ask in the room?

## In Scope

- Ranking 5-10 realistic provider targets within one territory slice
- Surfacing provider-specific why-now context
- Turning CRM notes into objection-aware prep
- Generating editable, evidence-grounded drafts for objection handling and meeting scripts
- Showing the public Tempus evidence used for each generated output

## Out Of Scope

- CRM write-back
- autonomous outreach
- treatment recommendation
- open-ended general chat
- enterprise auth, admin tooling, or production integrations

## Success Criteria

- A rep can identify a priority account in under 60 seconds.
- The reasoning behind ranking is deterministic and explainable.
- Generated outputs are grounded in public Tempus facts plus mock CRM context.
- The draft sounds like a rep preparing for a conversation, not a marketing brochure.
- The output suggests a concrete next ask instead of generic product praise.
