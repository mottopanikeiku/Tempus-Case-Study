# Scoring Rubric

## Purpose

Provider ranking is deterministic so the rep can audit why a provider appears near the top of the list.

## Weighted Factors

- `25%` Territory size
  Uses estimated genomic-eligible patients relative to the territory.
- `20%` Whitespace
  Measures the gap between eligible opportunity and current Tempus volume.
- `20%` Readiness
  Uses CRM momentum, latest sentiment, and whether a next step is already defined.
- `15%` Influence
  Rewards institutional leverage and local decision-making power.
- `10%` Why now urgency
  Rewards recent momentum, pilots, contract windows, tumor-board moments, and active follow-up windows.
- `10%` Account fit
  Rewards structured disease, stakeholder, payer, and incumbent-matchup patterns where Tempus has a clearer commercial story.

## Role-Specific Adjustments

### Lab Directors And Pathology Leaders

- Receive the highest influence weighting because they often control routing.
- Account fit increases when pricing, routing, RNA integration, or contract timing are relevant.
- Recommended asks focus on pilots, routing shifts, proposals, and enterprise timing.

### Medical Directors And Program Leads

- Influence remains high because they shape service-line workflow and broader adoption.
- Why-now signals are stronger when tumor-board visibility, pilot design, or cross-team adoption is possible.

### Treating Clinicians

- Readiness and provider-specific objections matter more in practice.
- Account fit increases when disease mix aligns with Tempus DNA, RNA, liquid-biopsy, or hereditary workflows.
- Recommended asks focus on the next few real patients, a narrow pilot, or a disease-specific follow-up.

## Explainability Standard

A ranked provider should always have:

- a visible score
- a breakdown of contributing factors
- a short why-now explanation
- a concrete next step
- clear language that `Account fit` is deterministic from structured local fields rather than model judgment
