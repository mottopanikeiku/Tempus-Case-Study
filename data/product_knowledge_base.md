# Tempus Product Knowledge Base

Public-source working notes used to ground the Tempus Sales Copilot prototype.

## xT CDx

- Type: FDA-approved 648-gene tissue-based molecular profiling test for malignant solid tumors
- Workflow: tumor plus normal matching
- Typical timing: public patient-facing Tempus oncology materials describe genomic profiling workflows as typically reporting in about 10-14 days after required specimens are received
- Use cases: broad solid tumor profiling, tissue-first workflows, therapy and trial context
- Sources:
  - https://www.tempus.com/news/tempus-receives-u-s-fda-approval-for-xt-cdx-a-ngs-based-in-vitro-diagnostic-device/
  - https://www.tempus.com/patients/oncology/
  - https://www.tempus.com/oncology/genomic-profiling/

## xR

- Type: whole-transcriptome RNA sequencing for solid tumors and hematologic malignancies
- Key fact: reports clinically relevant fusions across more than 100 targeted genes
- Key fact: public Tempus materials say DNA + RNA sequencing identified 21% more patients with driver fusions eligible for FDA-approved targeted therapies than DNA sequencing alone
- Use cases: thoracic oncology, sarcoma, pediatric tumors, thyroid, rare tumors, heme contexts where fusion biology matters
- Source:
  - https://www.tempus.com/oncology/genomic-profiling/xt-xr/
  - https://www.tempus.com/oncology/genomic-profiling/

## xF and xF+

- Type: liquid biopsy for advanced solid tumors
- Panel sizes: xF is 105 genes; xF+ is 523 genes
- Workflow: public xF clinical overview specifies two Streck tubes for xF
- Typical timing: Tempus positions xF results in about 7 days and xF+ in about 7-9 days after specimen retrieval
- Use cases: inaccessible tissue, liquid-first workflows, resistance tracking, monitoring
- Sources:
  - https://www.tempus.com/oncology/genomic-profiling/xf/
  - https://www.tempus.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/Tempus-NYS-xF_Clinical-Overview.pdf

## Hereditary Testing Powered By Ambry

- Type: hereditary and germline cancer testing through Ambry Genetics, a Tempus company
- Panels: CancerNext 40-gene and CancerNext-Expanded 77-gene options with optional RNAinsight
- Timing: approximately 5-14 days after specimen receipt
- Use cases: breast, ovarian, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, Lynch syndrome assessment
- Source:
  - https://www.tempus.com/oncology/genomic-profiling/hereditary/

## TIME Trial Network

- Role: clinical-trial matching and operational trial support
- Key fact: Tempus public clinical-development materials highlight 5M+ cancer patients covered by the TIME network and around 100 participating sites representing 800+ clinical locations
- Use case: give reps a concrete next-step story when physicians ask what happens after the biomarker result
- Source:
  - https://www.tempus.com/life-sciences/clinical-development

## Reporting and Workflow

- Tempus positions its reporting workflow as connecting molecular findings to treatment and trial options rather than delivering a raw variant list alone
- Public materials also reference financial assistance resources and payer workflow support when coverage criteria are met
- Sources:
  - https://www.tempus.com/oncology/genomic-profiling/
  - https://www.tempus.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/Tempus-NYS-xF_Clinical-Overview.pdf
